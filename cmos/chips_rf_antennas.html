* indium phosphide (InP), gallium nitride (GaN), gallium arsenide (GaAs) ==> devices at <=40nm.


**Advantages**
1) Large bandwidth: => better data transfer rates (10Gbps compared to 1Gbit/s limit using microwave.
2) Small components sizes: example: half-wave dipole at 900MHz cellular frequency is ~6"; reduced to 2.5 mm at 60GHz in free space, less if on dielectric substrate.
3) Greater resolutions: narrow beam
4) Low interference, increased security: The narrow beam and short range can be a benefit since there is less interference from nearby radios. Much harder to intercept the signals; increased security - signal restricted to small areas.

**Challenges**
1) Limited range: short wavelength = transmission range of ~10 meters (low power apps). Range extended with high transmit power and antenna gains & receivers with high sensitivity.
2) wavelengths of 10mm- 1mm suffer high atmospheric attenuation; (fog, rain = highest, and shortening the transmission distances. All these factors reduce the possible range to about one kilometer.
3) Requires line of sight: Physical objects (trees, buildings) will block the waves.
4) Costly components: Manufacturing requires more precision = increasing costs.
